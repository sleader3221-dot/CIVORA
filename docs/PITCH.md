# 6-Minute Pitch and Demo

## 0:00–0:35 — Human opening

“At 10:22 this morning, six workers entered a hot MEP corridor. The temperature alone was not unusual. But combined with poor ventilation, task intensity, accumulated exposure, and one worker's fatigue risk, they were 18 minutes from an unsafe threshold. Today, most sites discover that too late.”

## 0:35–1:05 — Problem

Construction decisions are fragmented. Safety sees inspections, project managers see schedules, sustainability sees monthly spreadsheets, and tool teams see another system. Alerts lack context and action. The result is preventable risk, downtime, waste, and reporting nobody fully trusts.

## 1:05–1:25 — Solution

“Civora is the intelligence layer for the construction site. It connects live conditions, people, tools, and materials to help supervisors act before disruption becomes an incident.”

## 1:25–3:35 — Live demo

1. Show the Command Center and the heat alert.
2. Point to confidence, time-to-threshold, exposed workers, and recommendation.
3. Click **Dispatch response**.
4. Open the Digital Twin and select **MEP Corridor**.
5. Show temperature, air, noise, people, and two-hour forecast.
6. Open Safety Intelligence.
7. Explain the model factors and human-in-the-loop approval.
8. Open Carbon & Circularity and choose **AI optimized**.
9. Reserve the surplus plywood transfer.
10. Open Tools & Assets and schedule service for AG 4S-22.

## 3:35–4:15 — Technical depth

“Every module uses one typed operational graph. Site events can arrive from sensors, BIM, workforce systems, procurement, and connected tools. Edge processing removes personal data. Versioned events feed time-series models, constrained optimization, and an evidence-grounded copilot. Every recommendation carries confidence, contributing factors, source freshness, approval, and an audit record.”

Do not claim that the hackathon prototype has live production integrations. Say the current adapter is deterministic and reliable for demonstration.

## 4:15–4:50 — Business

Primary customers are general contractors and large subcontractors. Start with a paid pilot for heat-risk prevention and tool health, priced per active project. Expand through carbon, materials, workforce, reporting, and portfolio benchmarking.

Pilot success metrics:

- alert precision,
- median response time,
- heat exposure minutes,
- unplanned tool downtime,
- supervisor adoption,
- worker comprehension,
- measurable cost avoided.

## 4:50–5:25 — Impact

“Civora does not ask teams to choose between safety, productivity, and sustainability. A better-timed crew rotation protects workers and output. Preventive service protects uptime and asset life. Material reuse lowers carbon and cost. One operational decision can create all three outcomes.”

## 5:25–5:50 — Why now / track relevance

Tie the wording directly to the final official track statement. For a Hilti-aligned track:

“Construction is becoming connected, but connection alone is not intelligence. Civora turns connected tools and site signals into safer work, higher utilization, and circular value.”

## 5:50–6:00 — Close

“Construction should not need hindsight to become safer. Civora helps every site see sooner, decide better, and prove what changed.”

## Backup answers

- **AI validation:** prospective pilot, calibrated thresholds, precision/recall, intervention outcomes.
- **Privacy:** no face recognition, edge processing, role-based access, retention controls.
- **Integration:** stable domain contracts isolate vendor-specific adapters.
- **Offline:** installable PWA caches the operating shell and queues actions.
- **ROI:** avoided downtime, avoided incident exposure, asset life, material recovery, and reporting time.
