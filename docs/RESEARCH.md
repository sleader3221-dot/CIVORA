# Research and Winning Strategy

## Decision

Build for the construction technology track, with Hilti as the strongest sponsor fit, under the product name **Civora**.

The public event information supplied in the brief names Hilti judges but does not include the exact track problem statements. The team must verify the official statement when the Intended Track Form opens. Civora is intentionally framed broadly enough to adapt its first slide and KPI emphasis without changing the product.

## Why this problem

Construction sits at the intersection of the hackathon themes:

- **AI/ML:** multimodal risk detection, forecasting, explanation, and optimization.
- **Productivity:** tool utilization, schedule conflicts, crew allocation, and avoided downtime.
- **Social good:** worker safety, language inclusion, and heat-stress prevention.
- **Sustainability:** carbon budgets, waste diversion, circular materials, energy, and water.

This produces a stronger judging story than a single-purpose chatbot. Every feature contributes to the same decision loop:

> Sense → understand → recommend → approve → act → verify impact.

   Source: https://www.cidb.gov.my/eng/national-construction-policy-ncp-2030/

## User discovery hypotheses

Validate these with at least three construction professionals before pitching:

- Safety information is fragmented across inspections, radios, cameras, sensors, and spreadsheets.
- Supervisors receive alerts but lack context, priority, and recommended actions.
- Sustainability reporting happens after the work instead of shaping daily choices.
- Tool data, workforce readiness, and project planning live in separate systems.
- Multilingual briefings are hard to deliver consistently and verify.

Do not present these as validated facts until interviews are completed.

## Buyer and business model

**Primary buyer:** general contractors and large subcontractors.

**Daily users:** site managers, HSE leads, sustainability managers, tool coordinators, and project directors.

**Commercial model:**

- Platform fee per active project.
- Tiered fee by connected workers/assets/sensors.
- Enterprise connectors and reporting.
- Optional deployment/assurance services.

**Pilot wedge:** heat-risk prevention plus connected tool health on one project for 6–8 weeks.

**Expansion:** carbon, materials, workforce, and portfolio benchmarking.

## Defensibility

The defensible asset is not a generic language model. It is the site-specific operational graph connecting:

- zones and BIM objects,
- workers and competencies,
- tools and condition data,
- materials and passports,
- hazards and controls,
- interventions and measured outcomes.

Over time, this graph produces better local forecasts, reusable control playbooks, and reliable impact evidence.

## Judge objections

### “This is too broad.”

Answer: the product is broad, but the demo is one focused workflow: prevent heat stress without losing production. The other workspaces prove the same event model expands commercially.

### “Is the AI real?”

Answer: the interaction and decision architecture are real; telemetry and model outputs are deterministic demo data. A production pilot would connect verified inputs and validate models prospectively. We label this transparently.

### “Why not just use dashboards?”

Answer: dashboards describe. Civora prioritizes, explains, recommends, routes an accountable response, and measures whether the intervention worked.

### “What stops false alarms?”

Answer: confidence thresholds, sensor-quality checks, multi-signal confirmation, supervisor approval, feedback labels, and a full audit trail.

### “What about worker privacy?”

Answer: edge inference, no facial identity, minimum necessary retention, role-based access, anonymized coordinates, and worker consultation.
