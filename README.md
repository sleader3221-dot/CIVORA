# Civora

**Every site decision, safer, leaner, and lower-carbon.**

Civora is a judge-ready construction intelligence command center created for ImagineHack 2026. It unifies safety prediction, a live digital twin, carbon and circularity, workforce readiness, connected tools, and audit-ready reporting in one coherent workflow.

The prototype is deliberately honest: all current telemetry is deterministic demo data and is labeled as such in the interface. The architecture is designed so production sensor, BIM, workforce, procurement, and Hilti ON!Track-style integrations can replace the demo adapters without rewriting the product.

## Why this can win

- **Technical (30%):** strict TypeScript, shared event model, live simulation, persistent state, PWA/offline support, code splitting, predictive workflows, and automated tests.
- **Content (20%):** a painful, valuable construction problem with a credible contractor/developer buyer and measurable ROI.
- **Pitch (20%):** one memorable story: detect a heat risk, explain it, dispatch a response, protect output, and prove the impact.
- **Design (15%):** responsive command-center UX, accessibility states, dark/light themes, spatial twin, and polished feedback.
- **Track relevance (10%):** directly connects construction digitalization, worker safety, tool productivity, and circular construction.
- **Growth (5%):** edge AI, explainability, material passports, carbon accounting, spatial operations, and multilingual worker UX.

## Judge demo path

1. Start at **Command center** and state the problem in 15 seconds.
2. Dispatch the critical heat intervention.
3. Open **Live digital twin** and select the MEP Corridor.
4. Open **Safety intelligence** and explain the five risk factors.
5. Open **Carbon & circularity**, switch to **AI optimized**, and reserve the material transfer.
6. Open **Tools & assets**, schedule preventive service for the drifting grinder.
7. Open **Impact reports** and export the transparent evidence report.
8. Ask **Civora Copilot**: “Find carbon savings.”

## Important positioning

Civora is **decision support**, not an autonomous safety authority. A supervisor approves every operational recommendation. The prototype does not claim certified carbon accounting, safety compliance, or live camera inference. Production claims require connected, verified source data and domain validation.
